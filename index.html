About

Previously, I obtained my M.Sc. in Information Science from Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST), where I worked on estimating bone mineral density from X-ray images. My broader interests include image generation, medical image analysis, dataset distillation, and building reliable AI workflows that bridge research ideas with production environments.

Projects

A selection of research and engineering projects in medical imaging and self-supervised learning.

Bone Mineral Density Estimation from X-Rays

A deep learning pipeline for estimating bone mineral density from lumbar X-ray images by generating Digitally Reconstructed Radiographs (DRRs) and learning cross-modality mappings.

Designed a Pix2PixHD-based architecture with backbone and loss function modifications, trained on synthetic X-rays and evaluated on real clinical data.

  • Medical Imaging
  • Pix2PixHD
  • Image Translation
  • PyTorch
Research project at NAIST

Self-Supervised Pretraining for Breast Pathology

Self-supervised pretraining experiments on histopathology images using MMPretrain to improve downstream segmentation and scoring tasks in breast pathology.

Developed a PD-L1 CPS scoring pipeline that combines keypoint detection with traditional image processing to improve diagnostic consistency and reduce manual workload.

  • Self-Supervised
  • Histopathology
  • MMPretrain
  • Medical AI
Internship at West China Hospital
